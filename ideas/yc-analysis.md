# YC Analysis — Does Memor 2 Pass the Bar?

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Bae's honest verdict:** In the zone. Not yet obvious. The gap is 10 users who love it.

---

## What YC Actually Looks For

Not "a large number of people who sort of like it."
**"A small number of people who love it very much."**

Paul Graham: "It's better to have 100 people who love you than a million who sort of like you."

The nuclear reactor test: would anyone be devastated if Memor disappeared tomorrow?
Right now: No. Because Memor 2 doesn't exist yet.
After April 30: Maybe. That maybe needs to become a yes.

---

## What Passes

**Problem is real.** Developers are losing architectural ownership as AI writes their code. Proven today — Mukur built Memor and lost track of his own codebase.

**Problem is growing.** In 2024, 20% of devs used AI heavily. In 2026, 60%+. Every month this gets worse. Memor is on the right side of a wave.

**Insight is non-obvious.** Dropbox launched when people thought file sync was solved. Memor's insight — "you don't need to read AI code, you need to SEE what it built" — is not obvious. This is a YC green flag. Non-obvious insights with large markets are their sweet spot.

**Founder IS the customer.** Mukur built Memor, AI helped write it, and today said "I have no idea what's going on inside my own codebase." That is the product. YC loves founders who have the pain.

**Tight scope.** JS/TS only, <100k lines. YC likes this. Beachhead before expansion.

---

## What Does Not Yet Pass

**No daily users.** Zero people who would be devastated if Memor disappeared. This is the single biggest gap. Not a fatal gap — it closes after April 30 if the right 30 developers try it.

**Competition is the LLM itself.** When a developer doesn't understand their codebase, they ask Claude or Cursor to explain it. YC will ask: why won't Cursor add this canvas in 6 months?

The answer: Cursor sees files. Memor sees architecture. These are fundamentally different. A file-aware AI cannot tell you that Auth is coupled to Payment — it can tell you what's in the auth file. But this answer needs to be PROVEN, not argued.

**No one is screaming yet.** The architectural debt from AI code hasn't bitten most teams yet. The pain is building but not yet acute. Memor needs to ship before the pain peaks — be there when teams hit the wall.

---

## The Window

12-18 months before one of two things closes it:
1. Cursor/GitHub Copilot ships an architecture canvas (they have the distribution)
2. LLMs get good enough at on-demand codebase explanation that deterministic approach loses edge

April 30 is not a milestone. April 30 is the starting gun.

Problem. Market. Non-obvious insight. One sentence.
